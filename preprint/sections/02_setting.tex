\section{Setting and Estimands}\label{sec:setting}

The model score \(s:\cX\to[0,1]\) is fixed before the audit. We observe labeled source data \((X_i,Y_i)_{i=1}^n\sim P\) and target covariates \((X'_j)_{j=1}^m\sim Q_X\). Labels are binary, \(Y\in\{0,1\}\). The target labels may be unavailable at audit time.

\begin{assumption}[Covariate shift and overlap]\label{ass:shift-overlap}
The conditional label law is stable, \(P(Y\given X)=Q(Y\given X)\), and \(Q_X\ll P_X\). The density ratio
\[
w(x)=\frac{dQ_X}{dP_X}(x)
\]
is finite \(P_X\)-almost surely.
\end{assumption}

Let \(\cG\) be a finite family of subgroup indicators and let \(\Bin=\{I_1,\ldots,I_B\}\) be a partition of the score range. A cell \(c=(b,g)\) is active when
\[
A_c(x)=\ind\{s(x)\in I_b\}g(x)=1.
\]
The finite audited family is \(\cC\), with \(M=|\cC|\).

For cell \(c\), the target mass and target residual mass are
\[
\mu_c^Q=\EE_Q[A_c(X)],
\qquad
\theta_c^Q=\EE_Q[(Y-s(X))A_c(X)].
\]
When \(\mu_c^Q>0\), the target cellwise residual is
\[
r_c^Q
=
\EE_Q[Y-s(X)\given A_c(X)=1]
=
\frac{\theta_c^Q}{\mu_c^Q}.
\]
This is the quantity behind a subgroup-bin reliability alarm. If \(r_c^Q>0\), outcomes are larger than the score in that cell; if \(r_c^Q<0\), outcomes are smaller.

Under \cref{ass:shift-overlap}, target moments are transported by importance weighting:
\[
\mu_c^Q=\EE_P[w(X)A_c(X)],
\qquad
\theta_c^Q=\EE_P[w(X)(Y-s(X))A_c(X)].
\]
The self-normalized estimator of \(r_c^Q\) is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rhat-main}
\widehat r_c(w)
=
\frac{\sum_i w(X_i)A_c(X_i)(Y_i-s(X_i))}
{\sum_i w(X_i)A_c(X_i)} ,
\end{equation}
whenever the denominator is positive. Its local effective sample size is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:local-ess-main}
{\ess}_c(w)
=
\frac{\bigl(\sum_i w(X_i)A_c(X_i)\bigr)^2}
{\sum_i w(X_i)^2A_c(X_i)}.
\end{equation}

The global effective sample size,
\[
{\ess}_{\mathrm{global}}(w)
=
\frac{(\sum_i w(X_i))^2}{\sum_i w(X_i)^2},
\]
answers a different question: how much information remains in the full weighted sample. Worst-group auditing asks whether the selected local cell is supported.

Given a tolerance \(\tau>0\), a certified audit should not report only the largest point estimate. It should report a lower confidence bound on the excess gap:
\[
\operatorname{excess}_c
=
\left[|\widehat r_c|-B_c-\tau\right]_+,
\]
where \(B_c\) is a simultaneous uncertainty radius. A certified failure requires positive excess. A large \(|\widehat r_c|\) with zero certified excess is not a negative result; it is insufficient evidence.
