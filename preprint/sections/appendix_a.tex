\section{Appendix}

\subsection{Proof of \cref{prop:transport}}

We prove the two transported moments and then divide them to obtain the ratio form for \(r_{b,g}^Q\).

For the cell mass, apply the standard change-of-measure identity with
\[
h(x,y)=A_{b,g}(x).
\]
Then
\[
\EE_P[\wt(X)A_{b,g}(X)]
=
\EE_Q[A_{b,g}(X)]
=
\mu_{b,g}^Q.
\]

For the residual mass, choose
\[
h(x,y)=(y-\score(x))A_{b,g}(x).
\]
The same identity gives
\[
\EE_P[\wt(X)(Y-\score(X))A_{b,g}(X)]
=
\EE_Q[(Y-\score(X))A_{b,g}(X)]
=
\theta_{b,g}^Q.
\]

If \(\mu_{b,g}^Q>0\), then \cref{eq:r-def} implies
\[
r_{b,g}^Q
=
\frac{\theta_{b,g}^Q}{\mu_{b,g}^Q}
=
\frac{\EE_P[\wt(X)(Y-\score(X))A_{b,g}(X)]}{\EE_P[\wt(X)A_{b,g}(X)]}.
\]

\subsection{Proof of \cref{thm:local-ess}}

Fix a subgroup-bin cell \((b,g)\) and abbreviate \(A_i=A_{b,g}(X_i)\), \(\wt_i=\wt(X_i)\), and \(\score_i=\score(X_i)\). Conditional on \(X_{1:n}\), the denominator
\[
S_A=\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_iA_i
\]
is deterministic and positive by assumption. Therefore
\[
\widehat r_{b,g}
=
\sum_{i=1}^{n}\alpha_i(Y_i-\score_i),
\qquad
\alpha_i=\frac{\wt_iA_i}{S_A}.
\]
So \(\widehat r_{b,g}\) is a weighted average with normalized weights \(\alpha_i\).

Taking conditional expectation gives
\[
\EE[\widehat r_{b,g}\given X_{1:n}]
=
\sum_{i=1}^{n}\alpha_i\,\EE[Y_i-\score_i\given X_i]
=
\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_iA_i\,\EE[Y_i-\score_i\given X_i]}{\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_iA_i}
=
\bar r_{b,g}(X_{1:n}).
\]

Because the observations are conditionally independent given the features,
\[
\Var(\widehat r_{b,g}\given X_{1:n})
=
\sum_{i=1}^{n}\alpha_i^2\Var(Y_i-\score_i\given X_i).
\]
Substituting the definition of \(\alpha_i\) yields
\[
\Var(\widehat r_{b,g}\given X_{1:n})
=
\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_i^2A_i^2\Var(Y_i-\score_i\given X_i)}{\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_iA_i\right)^2}.
\]
Since \(A_i\in\{0,1\}\), we have \(A_i^2=A_i\), so
\[
\Var(\widehat r_{b,g}\given X_{1:n})
=
\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_i^2A_i\Var(Y_i-\score_i\given X_i)}{\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_iA_i\right)^2}.
\]
Using the bound \(\Var(Y-\score(X)\given X=x)\le \sigma_{b,g}^2\) on the active cell gives
\[
\Var(\widehat r_{b,g}\given X_{1:n})
\le
\sigma_{b,g}^2\,
\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_i^2A_i}{\left(\sum_{i=1}^{n}\wt_iA_i\right)^2}
=
\frac{\sigma_{b,g}^2}{{\ess}_{b,g}}.
\]
This proves \cref{thm:local-ess}.

\subsection{Proof of \cref{cor:false-alarm}}

Under the additional assumption in \cref{cor:false-alarm}, we have
\[
\EE[Y_i-\score_i\given X_i]=0
\]
whenever \(A_i=1\). By \cref{thm:local-ess},
\[
\EE[\widehat r_{b,g}\given X_{1:n}]=0
\]
for every audited cell with positive denominator. Chebyshev's inequality therefore gives
\[
\PP\!\left(|\widehat r_{b,g}|>t\middle|X_{1:n}\right)
\le
\frac{\Var(\widehat r_{b,g}\given X_{1:n})}{t^2}
\le
\frac{\sigma_{b,g}^2}{t^2{\ess}_{b,g}}.
\]
Applying a union bound over all \(B|\cG|\) audited cells yields \cref{eq:false-alarm-bound}. The second display in the corollary follows by replacing each local ESS by the smallest one and each variance bound by \(\sigma_{\max}^2\).

\subsection{Further explanation of \cref{prop:global-misleading}}

The counterexample is deterministic because the point does not depend on any stochastic subtlety. The sample can be globally well weighted and locally degenerate at the same time.

Take one active cell point with weight \(N\pi\) and \(N-1\) inactive points with equal weight \(N(1-\pi)/(N-1)\). Then:
\begin{itemize}
  \item the weighted target mass of the active cell is exactly \(\pi\);
  \item the active cell is supported by only one weighted point, so its local ESS is exactly \(1\);
  \item the full sample still spreads most of its mass across many inactive points, so the global ESS can remain large.
\end{itemize}

This construction is enough to show that a large global ESS does not rule out a statistically meaningless subgroup-bin estimate.

\subsection{Further explanation of \cref{prop:hidden}}

The hidden-failure construction and the local-ESS construction diagnose different problems.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \Cref{prop:hidden} is about \emph{real hidden harm}: opposite-sign subgroup errors cancel in the aggregate.
  \item \Cref{prop:global-misleading} and \cref{cor:false-alarm} are about \emph{false subgroup alarms}: the subgroup estimate is unstable because the cell has too little weighted information.
\end{itemize}

The final paper needs both statements. Without \cref{prop:hidden}, there is no reason to audit subgroups. Without \cref{cor:false-alarm}, there is no reason to distrust an unstable subgroup alarm.
